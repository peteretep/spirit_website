Sligo bay has a wide variety of wild life. On most sailings we encounter Gray Seals and Harbour Seals. Areas such as Culleenamore, Lissadell and the Seal rocks are among the favourite haunts of the seals in Sligo. In late winter you can see seal pups at Ardbolin Island, but they grow so quickly. By January they are nearly as large as their parents, but retain the white colour.

Sligo and Donegal bays are among the best whale and dolphin watching areas in Western Europe. Porpoises, Common Dolphins and Bottlenose Doplhins. In 2009 a group also spotted a huge Minke Whale. 2010 was a great year to spot Basking Sharks in the area.

There is also a never ending supply of sea birds. In 2011 we spotted our first Sea Eagle and were lucky enough to catch it on film! Who knows what wildlife 2012 will bring!

A trip on the MV Spirit is the best way to get out into the natural habitat of these magnificant beasts and the spacious deck area of the vessel serves as an ideal viewing platform when wild dolphins come out to play.
